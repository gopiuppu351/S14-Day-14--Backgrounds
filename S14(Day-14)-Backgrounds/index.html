Narendra Damodardas Modi

Narendra Modi is the 14th Prime Minister of India, serving since May 2014. Known for his strong leadership, he has spearheaded significant economic and social reforms, such as the Digital India campaign and the introduction of GST. A member of the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), Modi previously served as the Chief Minister of Gujarat from 2001 to 2014. He is recognized for his emphasis on nationalism, development, and promoting India on the global stage. Modi's governance style is characterized by decisive policymaking and leveraging technology for governance and citizen welfare.

Nara Chandra Babu Naidu

Nara Chandrababu Naidu (CBN) is a prominent Indian politician and the President of the Telugu Desam Party (TDP). He served as the Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh for three terms, known for his focus on technology-driven governance and economic reforms. Naidu played a significant role in developing Hyderabad as a global IT hub during his tenure. He is widely recognized for his administrative skills and efforts to attract investments to the state. Despite challenges, his vision for infrastructure and rural-urban connectivity remains a hallmark of his leadership.

Konidala Pawan Kalyan

Pawan Kalyan, an Indian actor-turned-politician, is a prominent figure in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. He founded the Jana Sena Party (JSP) in 2014 with a vision of fighting for social justice and good governance. Initially, he supported the BJP-TDP alliance during the 2014 elections but later distanced himself, criticizing their policies. In 2019, he contested independently, but his party failed to secure significant success. Despite electoral setbacks, Pawan Kalyan continues to advocate for public issues, focusing on unemployment, agrarian crises, and regional development.
